<p><p>Vaccines for non-infectious meningitis are available in various types, including polysaccharide vaccines, conjugate vaccines, combination vaccines, and Men B vaccines. Polysaccharide vaccines work by using molecules from the outer coating of the bacteria to stimulate the immune system. Conjugate vaccines link a small part of the bacteria to a carrier protein to enhance the immune response. Combination vaccines offer protection against multiple diseases in a single shot. Men B vaccines target a specific strain of bacteria causing meningitis.</p></p>
