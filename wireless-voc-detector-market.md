<p><p>The Wireless VOC Detector market includes various types of sensors for detecting volatile organic compounds (VOCs) in the air. Two common types are Photoionization Detectors (PID) and Metal-oxide Semiconductor sensors. PID detectors use ultraviolet light to ionize VOCs, while Metal-oxide Semiconductor sensors rely on a chemical reaction between VOCs and a heated metal surface. Both types offer portable, wireless solutions for monitoring air quality in industrial, commercial, and residential settings. They provide real-time data on VOC levels to ensure a safe and healthy environment.</p></p>
