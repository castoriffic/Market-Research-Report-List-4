<p><p>Electrochemical titrators are classified into three main types: potentiometric titrators, coulometric titrators, and others. Potentiometric titrators measure changes in electrical potential during titration, providing precise endpoint detection. Coulometric titrators use a controlled electric current to quantify the amount of material in a solution, offering high sensitivity and accuracy. Other types of electrochemical titrators may include amperometric titrators or voltammetric titrators, which measure current or potential changes during titration. Each type serves specific purposes in analytical chemistry applications.</p></p>
