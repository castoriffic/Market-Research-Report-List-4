<p><p>Non-invasive ventilation machines, such as CPAP and BiPAP machines, are used to help individuals with breathing difficulties without the need for intubation. CPAP machines provide a continuous stream of pressurized air to keep airways open, while BiPAP machines offer varying levels of pressure for inhalation and exhalation. Both types of machines are commonly used for conditions like sleep apnea, COPD, and respiratory failure. The market for these machines continues to grow as more individuals seek non-invasive treatment options for their respiratory issues.</p></p>
