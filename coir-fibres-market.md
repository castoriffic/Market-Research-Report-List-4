<p><p>Coir fibers are categorized into two main types: brown fibers and white fibers. Brown fibers are obtained from fully mature coconuts and tend to be stronger and more durable, making them suitable for applications requiring higher strength. White fibers, on the other hand, are obtained from younger green coconuts and are softer and more flexible, making them ideal for uses where flexibility is important. Both types of coir fibers have their own distinct market segments based on their specific characteristics and applications.</p></p>
