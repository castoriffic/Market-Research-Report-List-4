<p><ul><li>Natural Cellulose Fibers</li><li>Man-Made Cellulose Fibers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Finished cellulose fiber markets are divided into two main types: natural cellulose fibers and man-made cellulose fibers. Natural cellulose fibers are derived from plants such as cotton, hemp, and flax, and are known for their breathability and biodegradability. Man-made cellulose fibers, on the other hand, are chemically processed to create fibers like viscose and lyocell, which offer properties such as moisture-wicking and durability. Both types of cellulose fibers are widely used in textiles, apparel, and nonwoven applications.</p></p>
