<p><p>Native cassava starch comes in two main market types: amylose and pullulan. Amylose cassava starch is known for its high amylose content, which provides desirable properties such as thickening and gelling. On the other hand, pullulan cassava starch is a unique variety that offers excellent film-forming and encapsulation properties. Both types of cassava starch are popular choices in various industries, including food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics, for their functional and versatile characteristics.</p></p>
