<p><p>Microbial agricultural inoculants play a crucial role in enhancing soil fertility and promoting plant growth. Soil inoculation involves the direct application of beneficial microorganisms to the soil, leading to improved nutrient uptake and plant health. On the other hand, seed inoculation refers to the coating of seeds with microbial inoculants before planting, ensuring the presence of beneficial microorganisms at the seed germination stage. Both methods are key components of sustainable agriculture practices, contributing to increased yields and reduced dependency on chemical fertilizers.</p></p>
