<p><p>Synchronous machines are classified into two main types based on their excitation methods: Electric Excitation Synchronous Motors and Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motors. The Electric Excitation Synchronous Motors rely on an external power source to produce the magnetic field needed for operation, while Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motors use built-in permanent magnets to generate the necessary magnetic field. Both types are widely used in various industrial applications due to their efficient synchronous operation and high performance capabilities.</p></p>
