<p><p>Bionic exoskeletons are classified into two main types: active exoskeletons and passive exoskeletons. Active exoskeletons are powered by motors and require energy input to assist with movement and support of the wearer. On the other hand, passive exoskeletons do not have motors and operate through the use of springs and other mechanical components to provide support and improve mobility. Both types of exoskeletons have their own unique applications and benefits in the market.</p></p>
