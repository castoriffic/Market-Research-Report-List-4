<p><p>Fixed firefighting systems include gaseous and liquid systems, as well as other types such as foam, powder, and water-based systems. Gaseous systems use agents like CO2 or FM200 to suppress fires by reducing oxygen levels or cooling the fire. Liquid systems use water or other chemicals to extinguish fires through cooling or smothering. Other systems, like foam or powder, are also used to suppress fires by smothering or inhibiting chemical reactions. Each system offers unique advantages for different fire hazards and environments.</p></p>
