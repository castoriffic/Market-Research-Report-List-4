<p><p>Urea-Formaldehyde (UF) adhesive resins of woods are classified into Type I and Type II markets based on their formaldehyde emission levels. Type I resins have lower formaldehyde emissions, making them more environmentally friendly and suitable for interior applications where formaldehyde exposure must be minimized. Type II resins have higher formaldehyde emissions and are typically used for exterior applications where formaldehyde emissions are less of a concern. Both types offer strong bonding properties for wood products, but Type I resins are preferred for more sensitive indoor environments.</p></p>
