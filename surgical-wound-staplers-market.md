<p><p>Surgical wound staplers are categorized into two main types: disposable and non-disposable. Disposable staplers are single-use devices that are disposed of after a single procedure, reducing the risk of cross-contamination and infection. Non-disposable staplers, on the other hand, are reusable devices that require sterilization between uses. Disposable staplers are typically more convenient and cost-effective for hospitals and healthcare facilities, while non-disposable staplers may be preferred for their durability and sustainability. Both types play a crucial role in wound closure and healing in surgical procedures.</p></p>
