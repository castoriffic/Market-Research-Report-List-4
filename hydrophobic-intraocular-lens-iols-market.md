<p><p>Hydrophobic intraocular lenses (IOLs) can be categorized into two main types: pre-loaded IOLs and not pre-loaded IOLs. Pre-loaded IOLs come already loaded in an injector, making the insertion process simpler for surgeons. On the other hand, not pre-loaded IOLs require manual loading by the surgeon during the procedure. Both types of IOLs offer the benefits of hydrophobic material, which repels water and reduces the risk of post-operative complications such as inflammation and cloudiness.</p></p>
